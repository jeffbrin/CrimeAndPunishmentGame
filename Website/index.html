About Crime and Punishment

Author & Description

Crime and Punishment is a novel written by the Russian author Fyodor Dostoevsky, first published in 1866. It was written by Dostoevsky after he returned from ten years of exile in Siberia, where he was sent for being critical of Tsarist Russia. The novel is often cited as being one of the most influential ever published, and one of the biggest achievements in world literature.


The story follows Rodion Raskolnikov, an ex-student, currently living in poverty in 19th century St. Petersburg. In debt, and with no money left to pay off what he owes, Raskolnikov divises a plan to murder and rob a local pawn broker. He justifies his actions by arguing that the pawn broker is a leach on society, and that her money would be better off in his hands. The story follows the mental decline of Raskolnikov as he deals with the heavy toll of his actions as well as the impact it has on the people around him.

Game Progression

Progression of the game

  • Where it all started

    During a normal class, we were told we had a creative project on the novel and we should pick teams with people in our program. Computer Science being a pretty small program, we knew each other and quickly partnered up.

  • How it came to be

    With our new team ready to conquer the world, we took the first couple hours to come up with a plan and a fair division of work. With our fresh new vision, we started on our respective tasks.

  • The game

    With fresh artwork from a bundle, we started molding Dostoevsky's vision in Unity, a free to use game editing software. With a few hiccups here and there and a couple bugs, we were able to create a game we were all proud of.

  • The website

    In order to convey a finished product, we decided to make a website in order to host the game and help explain our creative decisions and ideas.
