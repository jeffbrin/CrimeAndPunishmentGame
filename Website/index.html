Crime and Punishment is a novel written by the Russian author Fyodor Dostoevsky, first published in 1866. It was written by Dostoevsky after he returned from ten years of exile in Siberia, where he was sent for being critical of Tsarist Russia. The novel is often cited as being one of the most influential ever published, and one of the biggest achievements in world literature.


The story follows Rodion Raskolnikov, an ex-student, currently living in poverty in 19th century St. Petersburg. In debt, and with no money left to pay off what he owes, Raskolnikov divises a plan to murder and rob a local pawn broker. He justifies this by arguing that the pawn broker brings no good to society, and that her money would be better off being used on himself. However, the murder takes a toll on his physical and mental health, and as detectives close come closer to cracking the case, he slowly slips into paranoia and delusion.
